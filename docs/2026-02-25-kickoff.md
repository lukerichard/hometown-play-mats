# Hometown Play Mats — Kickoff Brief (2026-02-25)

## Product Idea
- Customers capture their real neighborhood inside a Google Maps-style interface (search + drag + zoom).
- Output: a high-quality, neoprene/mouse-pad style play mat sized for toy cars (think Hot Wheels scale), featuring their own streets/landmarks.
- Personalization levers: street labels, home marker, custom text ("Emma's Neighborhood"), color themes.

## Target Customers
1. Parents of kids 3-8 who love toy cars/building sets.
2. Gift-givers (grandparents, moving gifts, baby showers).
3. Nostalgia/novelty buyers (college towns, wedding gifts, corporate swag for local companies).

## Differentiators vs generic car mats
- Hyper-personal: their block, not a fictional city.
- Premium tactile quality (stitched neoprene, vibrant UV print) vs thin vinyl.
- Optional digital download for Cricut/laser engraving.
- Potential AR tie-ins later (scan the mat to unlock stories).

## Open Questions
- Map data licensing + style pipeline (Google Maps Static? Mapbox? OpenStreetMap custom tiles?).
- Production partner for low-volume, high-quality mats (mouse-pad printers, sublimation vendors).
- Price point (likely $89-$149 depending on size) and margin after fulfillment.
- Shipping logistics for rolled mats.

## Immediate Next Steps
### Founder Strategy (Northstar)
- Craft the positioning narrative + 1-pager (problem, solution, audience, proof).
- Outline a validation plan (survey, landing page + waitlist, influencer outreach).
- Identify 5-10 comparable products (play mats, custom maps) w/ price + messaging analysis.
- Draft key questions for early customer interviews.

### Engineering (Forge)
- Technical spike: evaluate map providers for custom rendering + licensing costs.
- Sketch system architecture (frontend capture UI, rendering worker, order backend).
- List build/buy decisions (e.g., Mapbox Studio vs custom tile rendering pipeline).
- Inventory starter stack (Next.js? Svelte? Cloud hosting? print API integration?).
